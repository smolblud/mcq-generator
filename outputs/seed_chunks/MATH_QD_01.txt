Square root basics: sqrt(25) = 5 because 5^2 = 25. Solving x^2 = 36 yields x = ±6 since both 6 and -6 square to 36. For (x-2)^2=49, take square roots: x-2 = ±7 so x = 2 ± 7 giving x = 9 and x = -5.
The ± symbol indicates the two possible square roots for a positive number.
