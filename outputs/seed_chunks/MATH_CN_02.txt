Squaring pure imaginary numbers shows how they relate to real numbers. Example: (3i)^2 = 3^2 i^2 = 9 i^2 = 9(-1) = -9.
Hence 3i is a square root of -9. In general, for a>0, sqrt(-a) = i sqrt(a), so sqrt(-9) = 3i, sqrt(-5) = i sqrt(5), etc.
notes: squaring pure imaginary numbers; sqrt(-a)=i sqrt(a)
